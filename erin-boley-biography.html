Who is Erin Boley?

Erin Boley is a talented American professional basketball player, who plays for the Oregon Ducks women’s basketball team on behalf of the University of Oregon.

In addition, she is the winner of the Silver Medal in the 2015 FIBA 3×3 U18 World Cup.

She was born as a daughter of her mother, Mary Beth, and Father, Scott Boley.

Her father, Scott was a former basketball player who played for Western Kentucky. Likewise, she has two brothers named Jacon Boley and Cutter Boley.

She shares a good bond with her parents.

Career, Professional Life

Erin started her career as an athlete at Elizabethtown High School.

At that time, she was named the ‘Gatorade High School Basketball Player of the Year’.

However, Boley professionally began her career at Notre Dame as a freshman, in 2016. She appeared in all 37 games for Notre Dame as a true freshman in the 2016-17 season.

Erin shot 39.5 percent (47-of-119) from three-point range putting her the second-best on the squad.

She then transferred to Oregon the following year for the 2017–2018 season but wasted her one year due to transfer rules. She finished her college career with 1,025 points that putting her at No. 32 on the program’s all-time list.

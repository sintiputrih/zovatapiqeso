Manilow’s successful career as a Singer-Songwriter

After “Mandy,” Manilow released a number of popular songs that topped the charts for an extended period of time.

His heartwarming songs, “Tryin’ to Get the Feeling,” “This One’s For You,” and “Indeed Now,” were featured on multi-platinum albums that won over listeners over.

He rose to fame as an entertainer after starring in the 1985 television film “Copacabana,” which was a tribute to his hit song of the same name.

Manilow’s Collaborations and Songwriting Career

Manilow was a terrific songwriter in addition to a superb vocalist. Many of his well-known singles, such as “Could it Be Magic,” “Weekend in New England,” and “Ships,” were written by him.

In addition, he wrote songs for other musicians like Dionne Warwick and Bette Midler.

He has collaborated with musicians such as Ricky Martin, Bruce Johnston, Bob Gaudio, and Billy Joel.

Manilow’s Commercial Jingles

Writing commercial jingles for well-known businesses was Manilow’s source of income prior to his rise to fame as a singer-songwriter.

His catchy commercials for Pepsi (“Feelin’ Free”), KFC (“Grab a Bucket of Chicken”), and State Farm (“Like a Good Neighbor”) are all well-known.

Additionally, people still recall McDonald’s (“You Deserve a Break Today”).

Nevertheless, as his jingles were all one-time, paid contract jobs, Manilow was not compensated for any residuals from them.

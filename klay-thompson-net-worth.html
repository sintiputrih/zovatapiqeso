Klay Thompson Net Worth 2022: How Rich Is the Golden State Warriors Star?

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Thursday, August 8, 2024

Klay Thompson Net Worth 2022: Klay Alexander Thompson is an NBA professional basketball player for the Golden State Warriors (NBA).

He is widely considered to be one of the best shooters in NBA history. Klay is a three-time NBA champion, a five-time NBA All-Star, and a two-time All-NBA Third Team selection. He was also chosen to be on the NBA’s Second All-Defensive Team.

In this article, we will explore the net worth of Klay Thompson.

Early Life of Klay Thompson

Klay Thompson was born in 1990 in Los Angeles, California, to Julie Thompson, a collegiate volleyball player, and Mychal Thompson, an NBA player. Michel, his older brother, and Trayce, his younger brother, are his siblings.

Thompson and his family moved to Lake Oswego, Oregon, when he was two years old, where he was boyhood friends with future NBA player Kevin Love.

He later moved to Ladera Ranch, California, when he was 14, to attend Santa Margarita Catholic High School. He guided his high school basketball team to a 30-5 record and a trip to the Division III State Championship as a senior.

Thompson set a state finals record by making seven three-pointers in a single game in the championship. He was voted Division III State Player of the Year and League MVP after that.

Klay went to Washington State University for college. He started all 33 games as a freshman for the basketball team and was chosen to the Pac-10 All-Freshman Team.

He led the Cougars to the Great Alaska Shootout Championship in his sophomore season, where he was voted Most Outstanding Player.

After becoming the third-fastest player in Cougars history to reach 1,000 points, he was named to the All-Pac-10 First Team.

Thompson led the Pac-10 in scoring as a junior in 2011, earning All-Pac-10 first-team honors. He set a tournament record with 43 points and eight three-pointers during the Pac-10 tournament.

He concluded the season with 733 points, the most ever scored in a single season by a Washington State player.

Klay Thompson Career

Thompson declared for the 2011 NBA draught after his junior season, and the Golden State Warriors selected him 11th overall.

He had a good start to the year 2013. Against the ‘Cleveland Cavaliers,’ he set a season-high with 32 points. He formed the top shooting partnership in NBA history with Stephen Curry, scoring 483 three-pointers, the most ever by an NBA duo.

Thompson and Curry set a new career-high in 2015, making 525 three-pointers and breaking their previous mark by 41 points.

He set a season-high with 45 points against the ‘Dallas Maverick’ the following year. ‘Western Conference All-Star’ reserve for the 2016 ‘NBA All-Star Game,’ earning his second straight ‘All-Star’ nod.

For the first time in his career, Thompson scored two straight 40-pointers against the ‘Dallas Mavericks and the ‘Philadelphia 76ers in March. Later in his career, he became the first player in NBA history to hit at least seven three-pointers in consecutive playoff games. Klay Thompson is set to retire in 2022.

Klay Thompson’s Net Worth

Thompson’s net worth is estimated to be $70 million in 2022 by Celebrity Net Worth. Klay derives a large portion of his net worth from his NBA contract, but Forbes reported last year that the shooting guard earned $14 million per year from sponsorships.

Klay Thompson Source of Income

Contracts: Klay Thompson has agreed to a $190 million agreement with the Warriors, which would keep him with the team until 2024. Despite being sidelined for a long time due to injury, Thompson will be paid $37, 980,720 for the 2021-22 season.

Klay inked a four-year, $69 million contract with the Warriors in 2015. The contract paid an annual average of $17 million.

He then inked a five-year, $190 million contract with the Warriors in 2019. This corresponds to a $38 million annual compensation on average. It is the third-largest contract in NBA history as of this writing.

Klay earned $31 million in salary and sponsorships between June 2017 and June 2018. Endorsements brought in about $13 million of that total. He earned $40 million between June 2019 and June 2020.

Endorsements: Thompson has a slew of endorsement deals to his credit as a renowned personality in basketball. He is reported to have made $15 million in endorsements out of his total value of $34.3 million. He also supports brands such as Panini, BodyArmor, Bevel, NERF, and Gillette.

Who is Andrew Ng?

Andrew Ng, a Chinese-American computer scientist, specialized in machine learning and artificial intelligence.

Early life

Andrew Ng was born on April 18, 1976, in London, United Kingdom. He was born in Hong Kong to parents who were also immigrants. During his childhood, he spent the majority of his time in Hong Kong and Singapore and eventually graduated from Raffles Institution in Singapore in 1992. Ng is of Asian ethnicity and possesses dual Chinese and American citizenship. He graduated from Carnegie Mellon University in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, with a triple major in computer science, statistics, and economics in 1997, graduating at the top of his class. From 1996 to 1998, he worked on reinforcement learning and model selection research. Later, he discusses AT&T Bell Labs’ pick.

Andrew graduated from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in Cambridge, Massachusetts, with a master’s degree in 1998. He created the first publicly accessible, automatically indexed web-search engine for academic articles on the internet at the time. He got his Ph.D. in 2002 from the University of California, Berkeley, under the direction of Michael I. Jordan.

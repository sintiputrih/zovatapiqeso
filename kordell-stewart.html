Kordell Stewart- Biography, Wife, Age, Height, Net Worth, Career

Posted by Martina Birk on Tuesday, February 20, 2024

Kordell Stewart is a former American football player who spent 11 seasons in the National Football League (NFL), mostly with the Pittsburgh Steelers. He attended Colorado University and is most known for the “Miracle at Michigan,” a Hail Mary pass he completed to upset Michigan in 1994. He was given the moniker ‘Slash’ because his ability to play numerous positions other than quarterback, including wide receiver, running back, punter, and kick returner.

The Steelers drafted him in the second round of the 1995 NFL Draft. His most successful season was in 2001 when he led Pittsburgh to the top seed in the AFC and a return to the conference championship game while receiving Pro Bowl honors. With 38 rushing touchdowns, he ranks fourth among quarterbacks. He retired from the NFL in 2005. He has since been on other television shows, including Real Housewives of Atlanta and Deal or No Deal. He currently works as an analyst for ESPN and various radio broadcasts.

Kordell Stewart Biography

Kordell Stewart was born on October 16, 1972, in New Orleans, Louisiana, United States. He is of American nationality and of African-American ancestry. He had his 51st birthday in 2023. He is the son of Robert Stewart Sr. (his father) and Florence Stewart (his mother) (mother). He also has a younger brother, Robert Stewart Jr. His Zodiac sign is Libra, and he is a Christian.

Kordell Steward received his education at John Ehret High School in Jefferson Parish, Louisiana. In 1991, he was awarded a scholarship to the University of Colorado. Under coach Bill McCartney, he became one of the most prolific quarterbacks in school history. On September 5, 1992, he started his first game as a sophomore as Colorado launched a pass-oriented system that propelled the 12th-ranked Buffaloes to a 37–17 victory against in-state rival Colorado State. In the game, he completed 21 of 36 throws for 409 yards and four touchdowns, a then-school record. On September 24, 1994, he threw the game-winning 64-yard Hail Mary touchdown pass into the end zone to wide receiver Michael Westbrook in the final seconds to defeat the Michigan Wolverines.

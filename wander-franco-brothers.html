Wander Franco Brothers: Meet Wander Alexander And Wander Javier: Wander Franco, officially known as Wander Samuel Franco Aybar Sr is a Dominican professional baseball player.

He developed a passion for playing baseball at a younger age, has been consistent throughout his career, and has become one of the most sought-after baseball players.

At this time of writing (Monday, August 14, 2023), Franco is a professional baseball shortstop for the Tampa Bay Rays of Major League Baseball.

For minor leagues, he signed with the Tampa Bay Rays organization on July 2, 2017, and made his professional debut in 2018 with the Princeton Rays.

Prior to the 2019 season, Franco was ranked as the fourth-best prospect in baseball by Baseball America. He began the season with the Bowling Green Hot Rods.

He was promoted to the Charlotte Stone Crabs on June 25, 2019. Franco finished the 2019 season with a .327/.398/.487 slash line with nine home runs and 53 RBIs.

Prior to the 2020 season, Franco was ranked the No. 1 prospect in all of baseball. On June 20, 2021, the Tampa Rays announced that Franco would be promoted to the major leagues

He made his MLB debut on June 22, 2021. Franco and the Rays agreed to an 11-year, $182 million contract extension in November.

At age 20, Franco became the youngest player in baseball history to sign a contract worth at least $100 million

In 2022, Franco became the youngest player in Tampa Bay Rays franchise history to have a multi-homer game at 21 and 52 days old.

On July 4, 2023, Franco was selected for his first All-Star game as the roster replacement for Aaron Judge, who was injured. He represented the Dominican Republic in the 2023 World Baseball Classic.

Wander Franco Brothers: Meet Wander Alexander And Wander Javier

Wander Franco is not the only child of his parents; Nancy Aybar (mother) and Wander Franco (father).

He grew up with two other brothers, Wander Alexander Franco and Wander Javier Franco who played in the Houston Astros and San Francisco Giants organizations.

NFL Pick'em Pool Strategy, Confidence Rankings: Straight-Up Picks Week 18

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Thursday, September 12, 2024

Heading into Week 18, we have a 47-11 record in our top six overall for confidence purposes across the last 10 weeks.

On Saturday, Week 18 of the NFL season will begin with a pair of contests with massive playoff implications. The Ravens will host the Steelers at 4:30 p.m. ET, followed by the Texans against the Colts at 8:15 p.m. ET.

Below, we have our Week 18 pick’em pool strategy and confidence rankings!

NFL Pick’Em Pool Strategy Week 18

Cowboys (vs. Commanders)

The final week of the regular season brings with it a significant amount of ambiguity regarding who will play and who will sit, but the Cowboys seem to have a straightforward objective on Sunday – win and they secure the #2 seed and the NFC East division title. Dallas, needing only a win for their best-case scenario to manifest, has a favorable matchup against Washington, which has lost each of its last 7 games and 9 of their last 10 overall. and company should be motivated to take care of business, making them worthy of the top overall spot in our confidence selections this weekend.

Bills (vs. Dolphins)

From Week 14 to Week 17, the Bills ranked 4th in DVOA, while the Dolphins ranked 11th. The gap in talent in this matchup is likely even larger heading into Week 18, with the Dolphins losing Bradley Chubb and (likely) Xavien Howard for the regular season finale. Miami has already lost Jerome Baker and Jaelen Phillips on the defensive side of the ball in the last month, making it difficult to see how they intend to slow down a dynamic Buffalo offense on Sunday night. The Bills are playing elite football right now and are deserving of being favored in this matchup as they look to secure the #2 seed in the AFC playoff picture and another AFC East division title.

Bengals (vs. Browns)

The Bengals probably do not deserve to be favored by a full touchdown given that neither team has anything to play for in Week 18, but they should still be able to get the job done. Cleveland is sitting and quite a few other notable names in their season finale, and they have shown minimal interest in preparing to win this game, judging by their practice participation reports throughout the week. The Bengals are fighting to finish above the .500 mark, which has to count for something.

Packers (vs. Bears)

The Bears have already been eliminated from playoff contention, but that does not mean they won’t be extremely excited if they are able to keep their division rivals out of the playoffs with a win. Chicago ranks 4th in DVOA across the last month and has been, by all accounts, a truly formidable opponent during the second half of the regular season. Nevertheless, has played like an elite quarterback across the last two months and has the benefit of playing at home in Week 18. Green Bay is talented enough to find a way to win this contest in front of their home fans.

Buccaneers (vs. Panthers)

It’s never easy to win an NFL game, especially on the road, and even more so when facing an opponent that has absolutely no incentive to tank. The Panthers do not own their 1st round draft pick this upcoming summer, which means they have no selfish desire to lose this football game. Still, the Panthers have been in the same motivational seat 16 times this season and have only 2 wins, so it’s not as if they are all of a sudden going to be a difficult matchup. and company are coming off of a tough loss at home to the Saints in Week 17, but the team appears to be healthier heading into the season finale, which bodes well for their chances to secure a playoff berth with a win.

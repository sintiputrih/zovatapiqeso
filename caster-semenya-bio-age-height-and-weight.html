Caster Semenya Bio, Age, Height and Weight

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Monday, July 8, 2024

Caster Semenya, born on January 7th, 1991 in Pietersburg, South Africa, grew up and attended Nthema Secondary School and the University of North West, where she studied sports science.

In July of a certain year, Caster Semenya actively participated in the 2008 World Junior Championships in the 800 m event but did not qualify for the finals.

However, she achieved gold at the 2008 Commonwealth Youth Games, completing the race in 2:04.23.

In August of the same year, Caster Semenya emerged victorious in the 800 meters at the World Championships, securing gold with a time of 1:55.45 in the final, thereby setting the fastest time of the year.

In March 2010, Semenya faced a setback when she was denied the opportunity to compete in the local Yellow Pages Series V Track and Field event in Stellenbosch, South Africa.

This was due to the International Association of Athletics Federations (IAAF) delaying the release of its findings from her sex test.

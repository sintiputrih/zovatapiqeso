Corey Wieneke Murder and Dateline Is Annette Cahill Still In Jail?

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Sunday, April 7, 2024

Annette Cahill is serving a 50-year term in jail. Prosecutors claim she killed Corey Wieneke in West Liberty in 1992 using a baseball bat.

Cahill, also known as Annette Hazen, was then 29 years old and resided in the little Iowa town of West Liberty in 1992. She was the lead suspect in a cold case inquiry of Corey Wieneke’s killing some twenty-five years later.

Back then, Corey worked as a bartender in a pub owned by his family. Among the bar’s regulars, the young man was well-known.

After he and his fiancée, Jody Hotz, got engaged, the 22-year-life old’s looked to have a bright future. They were living together at the time of the occurrence.

Dateline Addresses Corey Wieneke Murder

At the age of 22, Corey Wieneke could only be considered a larger-than-life figure who attempted to use his celebrity in the small Iowa town of West Liberty for good.

He was “like a big brother to everyone in the area,” combining his relationship with fiancée Jody Hotz and his job as a bartender at his family’s Wink’s Bar & Grill.

That’s why, on October 13, 1992, the former high school football star was discovered dead in his own house, shocking his adoring community and the entire nation.

Jody was the one who hastily dialed 911 after arriving home from work on that fateful Tuesday, informing authorities that Cory “is all bleeding, and he isn’t breathing, and he is cold.”

The teen was meant to be in the pub, but instead, he was lying on the floor of the couple’s shared bedroom, dead from blunt force trauma.

It was subsequently discovered that he had been bludgeoned to death with an aluminum baseball bat, a likely premeditated crime given that nothing was taken from his home.

Is Annette Cahill Corey Wieneke’s Murderer In Jail?

Annette Cahill was put on trial for the first time in early 2019, but the jury could not reach a decision, and the case has ruled a mistrial.

Another witness testified at Annette’s second trial later that year that she saw Annette burn blood-stained garments a day or two after the murder in 1992.

Annette killed Corey in a jealous frenzy after seeing him with another woman, according to the prosecution. The prosecution claimed that there was no tangible evidence linking her to the crime.

The jury found Annette guilty of second-degree murder in the second trial. She faces a maximum sentence of 50 years in jail. Corey’s mother was pleased with the result.

“I haven’t even realized it’s done yet,” she remarked. We’re overjoyed, but it will take a few days for it to sink in, and I pity her family.” Annette has preserved her innocence throughout her life.

Corey Wieneke’s Parents

Corey Wieneke’s true name is Corey Lee Wieneke was born in Muscatine, Iowa, on March 25, 1970, to James and Susan Winfield Wieneke.

Susan and Jim Wieneke have been married for over 27 years. Corey, their only child, was murdered in the fall of 1992.

He graduated from West Liberty High School in 1988, when he was a member of the football team that reached the Class AA semifinals. He worked as a bartender at Wink’s Tap in West Liberty for numerous years.

After Corey’s death, not only was Jody ruled out as a suspect in the early hours of the inquiry, but the blood-stained murder weapon was also discovered on a lonely road about a mile from the crime site.

By telling when they saw or didn’t see the bat, witnesses helped narrow down the time frame of the crime to between 9 a.m. and 1 p.m. on that dark day.

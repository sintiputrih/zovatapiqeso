Nancy Grace Biography

Nancy Grace is an American journalist and legal commentator. She hosted a nightly celebrity news and current affairs show on HLN called by her name. She also hosted Court TV’s Closing Arguments from 1996 to 2007. Formerly, she was a prosecutor in a local district attorney’s office in Atlanta, Georgia.

She is the arbiter of Swift Justice with Nancy Grace in the syndicated courtroom reality show’s first season. Frequently, she discussed issues from what she describes as a victim’s rights standpoint. While she worked at the attorney‘s office, her work focused on felony cases involving serial murder.

Additionally, there was a time when she was reprimanded by the Supreme Court of Georgia for withholding evidence. It was also said that she had made improper statements in a 1997 arson and murder case. However, the court overturned the conviction in that case and found that she demonstrated her disregard for the notions of due process and fairness and was inexcusable.

Moreover, she began her media career by covering trials for the cable television network Court TV on the nightly show Closing Arguments. Gradually, she became known as an unrelenting interviewer who applied her prosecutorial skills to challenge her guest.

Furthermore, Grace attended Windsor Academy where she graduated in 1977. Later, she joined Valdosta State University and Mercer University where she graduated with a bachelor of arts degree. She was a fan of Shakespearean literature and intended to become an English professor after graduating.

Biography of Bruce Springsteen

Bruce Frederick Joseph Springsteen was born in Long Branch, New Jersey, on September 23, 1949, and grew up in Freehold. His younger sisters are two. Springsteen, who was raised as a Catholic and went to St. Rose of Lima Catholic School, attributes much of his musical influence to his upbringing.

However, Elvis Presley, who he watched on “The Ed Sullivan Show” when he was seven years old, was the one who initially sparked his interest in music. Springsteen did not participate in the Vietnam War because he failed the physical examination after sustaining a concussion in a motorbike accident when he was seventeen.

Early in his career, Bruce Springsteen played guitar for a number of groups, such as the Castiles, Earth, Steel Mill, and the Sundance Blues Band. In 1972, he formed the E Street Band and released his debut record, Greetings from Asbury Park.

With his third album, Born to Run, which peaked at number three on the Billboard 100 and launched a tremendously well-liked tour, he achieved mainstream success.

Born in the U.S.A., his 1984 album, has fifteen platinum certifications from the RIAA and is one of the best-selling albums of all time.

Springsteen launched a string of successful albums in the 1980s and 1990s, including Tunnel of Love, The Ghost of Tom Joad, and Human Touch. He later reformed the E Street Band in the 2000s.

Beginning with the Tom Cruise comedy Risky Business, which was released in 1983, his music has been used in numerous popular Hollywood films.

Adam Sandler’s film The Wedding Singer featured his song Hungry Heart, while Tom Hanks’ critically acclaimed movie Philadelphia had the appropriately named tune Streets of Philadelphia.

Is Bruce Springsteen married? Yes, Bruce Springsteen is married to Patti Scialfa. Patti Scialfa is an American songwriter and guitarist. They got married in 1991. However, Bruce first married Julianne Phillips from 1985 to 1989.
